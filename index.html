What's IPv6?

WHAT IS 'IP'?

IP stands for Internet Protocol and is used to identify the locations of devices across the Internet. Essentionally, the IP is similar to your mailing address so that a Christmas card finds your house, in that data from the Internet knows where to find you when you search.

Cisco, a worldwide leader in computer networking software and hardware, has shared this brief informative video on what an IP address is.

HOW DOES IT WORK?

IPv6 was established in 1998 by the Internet Engineering Task Force, and uses a 128-bit addressing scheme to support approximately 340 trillion trillion devices. This was a major upgrade from IPv4 which uses 32-bit addressing to support a mere 4.3 billion devices.

WHO USES IT?

Carrier networks and Internet Service Providers (ISPs) have been the first to begin deployment of IPv6 on their networks. Currently mobile networks are leading the charge, according to industry group World IPv6 Launch, T-Mobile USA has more than 92.90% of its traffic going over IPv6, while Comcast is at only 66.48%. Internet Society's State of IPv6 Deployment report says that software capability, complexity, costs, and time are all cited as reasons for companies trailing in deployment.
